ITON PLATFORM > INTERACTIVE MESSAGING

INTERACTIVE MESSAGING

nteractive messaging has become immensely popular since they allow people to reply to your SMS messages. A short code is a number between 3 and 7 digits in length that can be used by customers, suppliers and employees to reply to your messages. These are extremely useful for marketing campaigns or promotions. They are also ideal for customer interactions since they can easily remember the number.

Short codes are country specific and therefore cannot be used to receive replies from international locations. There are two types of short codes:

STANDARD SHORT CODES

These work across all major mobile operators. Additional fee is also not charged to mobile users when sending and receiving messages

PREMIUM SHORT CODES

These are mainly used by business organizations to generate revenue when competitions or promotional events are held. Mobile users will be charged extra when they receiving and responding to a message

ITON PLATFORM > U.S.S.D

U.S.S.D

With the existing competitive environment, customers prefer organizations that can successfully meet and even exceed their expectations. Real time connections can be created with your customers with Unstructured Supplementary Service Data (USSD). GSM cellular telephones use the USSD protocol to communicate with the service provider’s computers. The USSD protocol can be utilized for the following:

  • WAP browsing
  • Pre-paid callback service
  • Mobile money services
  • Location based content services
  • Menu based information services

USSD messages create a real-time connection with the user, which allows a two-way exchange of data. This makes it more responsive and an exceptionally fast connection is also established between the user and the application. It is therefore a very reliable option for any menu-based transaction or service.

ITON PLATFORM > N.F.C

NEAR FIELD COMMUNICATIONS

Technology is becoming more mobile as “on-the-go” lifestyles are becoming increasingly popular. Near Field Communications (NFC) is a set of standards that can be used to establish radio communication among smart phones and other similar devices. This is done by bringing the two devices into close proximity or by touching them together.

NFC has endless possibilities. Examples of applications include:

  • Instant payments and coupon delivery using handsets, as we currently do with credit and debit cards.
  • Marketing and exchange of information such as schedules, maps and business cards and coupon delivery using NFC Marketing tags
  • Payment for items by just waving the mobile phone over NFC capable devices
  • Transferring images and posters for displaying and printing via mobile phones
